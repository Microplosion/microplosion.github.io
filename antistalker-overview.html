Anti-Stalker Radar · Framework Overview

High-Level Behavioral Stalkerware Detection Framework

Anti-Stalker Radar is a signature-less, behavioral and attribution-based detection framework designed to identify covert mobile surveillance and stalkerware by correlating identity anomalies, permission clusters, lifecycle activity and multi-step behavioral sequences.

Inventor: Duane Michael Romualdo (Independent Researcher)
Domain: Mobile threat detection & forensic risk attribution
Problem Space
Why a new approach is needed

Modern stalkerware and covert surveillance apps are explicitly designed to evade traditional mobile security models:

  • Minimal permission sets and benign-looking manifests
  • Obfuscation, repackaging and piggybacking on legitimate apps
  • SIM / identity tampering and device-level instability
  • Silent re-enablement, rogue updates and persistence tactics

Static signatures, simple permission checkers and reputation lists are no longer sufficient to reliably surface these threats. What is required is a framework that reasons about intent, behavior and identity context over time.

Goal: attribute covert surveillance behaviour using correlated signals rather than chasing individual indicators of compromise.
Framework Summary
High-level only

Anti-Stalker Radar is a modular analysis layer intended to sit alongside existing mobile threat defense or consumer security products and provide:

  • Behavioral watchdog and lifecycle anomaly tracking
  • SIM and identity instability modeling for tampering signals
  • Permission-cluster forensic scoring (intent-focused, not raw counts)
  • Event-sequence correlation and risk delta evaluation
  • Attribution-ready outputs for UI, policy or automated response
Not a product by itself

The framework is designed as an intelligence engine that can be embedded, licensed or integrated into existing mobile security stacks.

Core Capabilities

Behavioral Watchdog
Continuously monitors lifecycle anomalies, hidden execution patterns, wake-lock usage, foreground service abuse and suspicious background behaviour following installation or updates.
Lifecycle anomalies Foreground / background abuse
Identity & SIM Forensics
Models expected stability of SIM / identity parameters and highlights suspicious transitions, instability or inconsistencies that align with SIM-cloning, number hijack or identity manipulation scenarios.
Identity anomalies SIM instability
Permission-Cluster Scoring
Evaluates clusters of permissions as forensic indicators (e.g. audio + location + call logs) and correlates them with observed runtime behaviour rather than treating each permission in isolation.
Intent-focused scoring Surveillance clusters
Rogue Update & Persistence
Tracks capability changes across install / update cycles, attempts to silently re-enable functionality after user action, and privilege expansions that do not match normal app evolution patterns.
Rogue updates Re-enablement attempts
Event-Sequence Attribution
Links low-level signals into higher-level narratives (e.g. SIM change → app re-enables itself → new background activity) and assigns risk deltas to each step to build attribution confidence.
Multi-step reasoning Attribution narratives
Risk Output & Tiers
Produces tiered outputs that can integrate with existing UI / policy: lightweight flags, elevated warnings or high-confidence alerts suitable for enforcement or user guidance.
Tiered outputs Low noise, high signal

Intended Use Cases

Anti-Stalker Radar is designed as an embeddable intelligence engine that can support multiple product directions, including:

  • Consumer mobile anti-stalkerware: surfacing covert surveillance behaviour to non-technical users with clear rationale.
  • Mobile threat defense solutions: adding behavioural attribution for high-confidence spyware and control-app detection.
  • Telecom / identity protection services: correlating SIM / identity anomalies with suspicious application behaviour.
  • Forensics and incident response: providing behavioural timelines and risk narratives for compromised devices.

Integration Philosophy

The framework is intentionally modular: it is designed to generate explanatory signals that can be consumed by existing analytics, UI and policy engines rather than replacing them.
