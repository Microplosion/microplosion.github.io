Behavioral Evidence Interpretation Layer (BEIL)

Many tools can raise an alert. Fewer can explain why it matters in plain language. BEIL is an interpretation layer that converts correlated device signals into a structured, readable output that can be reviewed later.

BEIL output focuses on:

  • Timeframe & timeline: what happened first, what followed, and why order matters.
  • Observed signals: the key events used for interpretation (with minimal sensitive content).
  • Interpretation: a clear explanation written for non-technical readers.
  • Confidence & rationale: what raised/lowered confidence, plus limitations and caveats.
  • Alternative explanations: benign possibilities considered, and why they may be less likely.

BEIL is designed to be additive: it operates on the outputs of a correlation engine rather than changing the underlying signal collection logic.
