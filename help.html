1. What Cache-Plosion Does

Cache-Plosion is a lightweight Android utility that helps free up storage by clearing cached data from your installed apps. It automates the same steps you would normally perform manually in Android Settings, using the Accessibility Service to tap buttons like “Storage & cache” and “Clear cache” after you start the cleaning process.

  • Helps reclaim storage space by clearing app caches.
  • Runs entirely on your device — no personal data is collected or uploaded.
  • Uses Android’s Accessibility Service only to automate cache-clearing steps you initiate.
Important: Cache-Plosion does not delete your personal files, photos, messages, or app data. It only clears temporary cached data that apps create.

2. Required Permissions

Cache-Plosion needs two permissions to work correctly. These allow the app to automate the same steps you would normally tap manually in Android Settings.

  • Usage Access — lets Cache-Plosion see your installed apps list so you can choose which caches to clear.
  • Accessibility Service — lets the app tap system buttons like “Storage & cache” and “Clear cache” on your behalf, only after you start the cleaning process.

2.1 How to Enable Usage Access

Step A — Tap “Open Usage Access” inside the app

This will take you directly to your phone’s App Usage Data settings page.

Step B — Scroll until you find “Cache-Plosion”

Under the name, it will show one of two states:
✔ Allowed — already enabled
✘ Not Allowed — needs to be turned on

Step C — If it says “Not Allowed”:

  1. Tap Cache-Plosion.
  2. Find Permit usage access.
  3. Turn the switch ON.

Step D — Return to the app

The Usage Access indicator will turn purple once it is enabled.

2.2 How to Enable Accessibility Service

Step A — Tap “Open Accessibility”

This opens your device’s Accessibility settings.

Step B — Look for “Cache-Plosion: Cache Cleaner Pro”

Step C — Turn the toggle ON

A system message will appear explaining what this service does. Cache-Plosion uses Accessibility only to automate cache-clearing steps you initiate. It does not read screen content, text, keystrokes, passwords, or personal data.

Step D — Return to the app

The Accessibility checkmark will turn purple once enabled.

Note: These permissions normally only need to be enabled once. If Android resets them after a system update or reboot, just repeat the steps above.

3. How to Use Cache-Plosion

  1. Open Cache-Plosion on your device.
  2. On the Permissions screen, make sure both Usage Access and Accessibility show as enabled (purple checkmarks).
  3. Tap “Continue to Cache Cleaning”.
  4. The app will display your apps and begin guiding the automated cleaning process.
  5. Follow any on-screen instructions if needed, then let the automation run.

During cleaning, Cache-Plosion uses Accessibility to open each selected app’s settings screen and tap “Storage & cache” and “Clear cache”, just as you would manually. All actions occur on your device and under your control.

4. Frequently Asked Questions

Does Cache-Plosion collect my personal data?

No. Cache-Plosion does not collect, store, or transmit personal or sensitive data such as names, emails, messages, photos, or passwords. All cache-cleaning actions happen locally on your device. For full details, see our Privacy Policy.

Why does the app need Accessibility?

Android does not provide a direct way for apps to clear other apps’ cache automatically. The only way is to open each app’s settings screen and tap the “Clear cache” button — the same way a user would. Cache-Plosion uses the Accessibility Service to automate those taps after you start the cleaning process.

Does the Accessibility Service read my screen or keystrokes?

No. The Accessibility Service used by Cache-Plosion is limited to performing navigation and taps required for cache cleaning. It does not read text, messages, passwords, or any personal content. It is not used as a keylogger or for tracking.

For a full explanation, see the Accessibility Service Disclosure.

Will this harm my apps or delete my files?

Clearing cache removes temporary files that apps create. It does not delete your photos, documents, messages, or main app data. Most apps recreate cache automatically as they are used.

Why did my permissions turn off by themselves?

On some devices, Android may reset Accessibility or Usage Access permissions after a system update, reboot, or battery optimization event. If this happens, open Cache-Plosion again and follow the permission steps to re-enable them.

Why do some apps still show cache after cleaning?

Some apps immediately start generating new cache as soon as you open them again. This is normal behavior. Cache-Plosion helps you remove existing cache, but apps may rebuild it based on how you use them.

5. Troubleshooting

The cleaner seems stuck or not moving to the next app

  • Make sure the Accessibility Service for Cache-Plosion is still enabled.
  • Ensure you are not interacting with other apps while the automation is running.
  • If the screen appears frozen, press the Home button, reopen Cache-Plosion, and try again.
  • Restart your device if the problem continues.

Buttons are not being tapped properly

  • Different Android versions and manufacturers may change the layout of Settings screens.
  • Make sure your device is using standard system layouts and that text size is not extremely large.
  • If the app cannot find the expected buttons, future updates may improve compatibility.

Accessibility keeps turning off

  • Some devices aggressively close background services to save battery.
  • Try disabling battery optimization for Cache-Plosion in your device settings.
  • If it turns off again, re-enable Accessibility and restart the app.
