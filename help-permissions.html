How to Enable Permissions for Cache-Plosion

Ok guys — this is simple. Cache-Plosion only needs two permissions to work:

  • Usage Access
  • Accessibility Service

Once they’re both on, the app can safely automate clearing the cache for every app on your device. Here’s exactly what to do:

1. Enable Usage Access

Step A — Tap “Open Usage Access” inside the app

This will take you directly to your phone’s App Usage Data screen in Settings.

Step B — Scroll down until you see “Cache-Plosion”

Under the name, it will show one of two things:
✔ Allowed — You’re good, no change needed
✘ Not Allowed — You need to turn it on

Step C — If it says “Not Allowed”:

  1. Tap Cache-Plosion
  2. On the next screen, find Permit access to app usage data
  3. Turn the toggle ON

Step D — Go back

Tap the back arrow (top left). You’ll see a checkmark under “Usage Access” on the app’s permission screen.

2. Enable Accessibility Service

Step A — Tap “Open Accessibility”

This opens your phone's Accessibility settings menu.

Step B — Look for “Use Cache-Plosion: Cache Cleaner Pro”

Step C — Turn on the toggle

A confirmation popup will appear explaining what the service does. Cache-Plosion only clicks buttons like “Storage & cache” and “Clear cache” in Settings.

Step D — Go back

Tap the back arrow again. You’ll see the Accessibility checkmark turn purple inside the app.

3. Continue to Cache Cleaning

Once both checkmarks are purple, the button at the bottom of the screen will unlock:

“Continue to Cache Cleaning”

Tap it — and you’re ready for the magic.

Note: You only need to enable these permissions once. If Android ever resets them after an update, just come back to this screen.
